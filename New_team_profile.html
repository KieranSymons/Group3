Team Composition

The breakdown of our teams’ personalities and tendencies will be a significant help in the smooth operation of the IT Mob. With the ability for each person to understand where the others are coming from, especially in this type of group work with limited contact and none face-to-face, any added information is helpful in reducing conflict and misunderstandings. Our team has a particularly good spread of personalities with very few clashes, and this will be an asset to our performance. Along with a few leadership type personalities we also contain an analytical-type architect and three mediator/emotional based personality type to keep the group running smoothly.

Our group also has a wide range of learning styles, this is an especially advantageous in the circumstances we are currently under as the variation in effective styles will allow our group to attain as much information as possible.

Group Processes

Below is an excerpt from the group reflection of our, the IT Mob’s, assessment two.

“Rarely do you see a group of six strangers mesh together so perfectly. The strong points outlined are consistent within everyone's reflections – communication, collaboration, willingness to help, willingness to share knowledge, and ability to agree on and complete an evenly shared workload. Realistically these traits would be the listed dot points of an ideal team player, and each member has ticked off every item. Throughout assignment 2 the IT Mob has come together on nearly every aspect of this assignment, whether it be through helping with proofreading, discussing document layouts, assisting with information collection, or correcting people’s referencing. As Angadh stated in his reflection - “Work was distributed equally according to individual's strengths which allowed for smooth workflow and dynamic collaboration between group members”, and this holds true not only for assignment sections but also covering each other's shortfalls in certain areas. 

Every single member found the above points surprising, and we hope we get to work together in the future. It’s also worth noting that while collaboration and assistance was given, everyone made incredible progress into their assigned sections before anyone else saw it.”

Upon reflection, our assessment of assignment two remains as it was upon completion. This is not to say the process we implemented were perfect, some of the notes for improvement raised by the group in the reflection again, are as follows.

“The improvements listed in our individual reflections were minor, and everyone picked up on different aspects that could be improved. These included time management, sharing work earlier and improving on skills and knowledge. All these improvements will be made naturally throughout our participation in this course and careers, and for our first group assignment, we believe the improvements to be minor and expected.”

Other improvements raised by group members that are to be implemented are:

  • Better management of proof reading by team members specifically assigned this task.
  • Keeping better track of tasks assigned to team members and implementing a task management system or program.
  • Uploading of work as it is completed rather than a single block at the due date.

By reflection upon our processes and their success or failure, we feel confident that we will be better placed in this Assignment 3. While incredibly pleased with our team's communication and work through the previous weeks, there are areas we would like to improve, and will implement the above improvement to benefit our performance going forward.

    Conclusion

    Our group has a diverse range of career aspirations. Many of the job roles contain similarities and many differences. The largest intersections in our jobs are the requirement for tertiary study with an allowable amount of industry experience as a substitute, and the need to be fluent in general computer related skills and applications as is to be expected. There is a large projected job growth across most of our jobs, with salaries that are all above the national average. The highlighted jobs of our group are quite different in their paths, several are management roles, several are technical roles, several are engineering while others are development. Overall, our groups interests are as widely varied as the members within it.

    In the discussion around our career plans, there is a sense of maturity in how we look at the industry compared to how we saw it before this course, Introduction to IT. The common theme amongst the plans outlined is the need for experience, competence, and a broad knowledge of the industry. It has become clear to all of us that the IT industry inherently requires its professionals to be able to learn and adapt to projects that include several different disciplines included, from a software architect requiring knowledge of skills such as programming through to business management and project development, to a mechatronic engineer whose skills need to include programming, science and creative problem solving. This aspect of the IT sphere is endemic, with the industry changing and evolving as fast as it is, being able to adapt and have broad ranging skills is necessary for a career in IT.
